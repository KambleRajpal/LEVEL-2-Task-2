A.P.J.Abdul kalam

Great Indian scientist and politicion who played a leading role in the development of India's missile and nuclear weapons programs.

About the

✦ A.P.J.Abdul kalam, in full Avul pakir jainulabdeen Abdul kalam, was born on October 15,1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu,India.

✦ He served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007.

✦ Kalam earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from the madras Institute of Technology and in 1985 joined the Defence Research and development organisation, where the was project director of the SLV-III, the first satellite launch vechicle that was both designed and produced in India.

✦ Rejoining DRDO in 1982, Kalam planned the program that produced a number of successful missiles, which helped earn him the nickname"missile Man"

✦ Among those success was Agni, India's first intermediate-range ballistic missile, which incorpoated acpects of the SLV-III and was launched in 1989.

✦ He also played a pivotal organisational, technical, and political role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1989, the first since the orginal nuclear test by india in 1974
